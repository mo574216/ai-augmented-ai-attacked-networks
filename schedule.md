# Course Schedule

## AI-Augmented and AI-Attacked Computer Networks

This page provides the weekly schedule for the course.  
Each week includes the main topic, key focus areas, and the intended practical or academic emphasis.

---

## Weekly Schedule

| Week | Topic | Main Focus | Practical / Academic Emphasis |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Introduction to AI-Augmented and AI-Attacked Computer Networks | Course overview, motivation, networking basics, AI basics, future directions | Building a shared conceptual foundation |
| 2 | Foundations of Machine Learning for Networking | Supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning; data, features, evaluation | ML thinking for networking problems |
| 3 | Network Telemetry, Data Collection, and Observability | Logs, metrics, traces, flow telemetry, streaming telemetry, observability pipelines | Understanding the data foundation of AI-driven networking |
| 4 | Traffic Classification and Application Identification | Port-based vs flow-based methods, ML pipelines, encrypted traffic classification | Practical traffic analytics |
| 5 | Deep Learning for Network Analytics | CNNs, RNNs, transformers, autoencoders, deep representations for traffic and security data | When deep learning helps in networking |
| 6 | Reinforcement Learning for Routing and Congestion Control | MDP formulation, reward design, RL for routing, congestion control, safety issues | Sequential decision-making in networks |
| 7 | AI for SDN and Network Resource Management | SDN architecture, traffic engineering, load balancing, admission control, AI-enabled control loops | AI as an operational control layer |
| 8 | Graph Learning for Networked Systems | Graph representations, GNN intuition, dynamic graphs, topology-aware learning | Structure-aware network intelligence |
| 9 | AI for Cybersecurity: IDS, Malware, and DDoS Defense | Intrusion detection, malware detection, DDoS analytics, security data, operational metrics | Cyber defense with AI |
| 10 | Adversarial Machine Learning in Networks | Evasion, poisoning, backdoors, AML threat models, LLM security risks | Attacking and defending AI systems |
| 11 | LLMs for Network Automation and Operations | Log summarization, config explanation, troubleshooting, retrieval, bounded tool use | LLM-based NetOps assistance |
| 12 | LLMs as Attackers and Defenders | Dual-use AI in cyber operations, offensive and defensive workflows, governance concerns | Understanding the cyber dual-use landscape |
| 13 | Trustworthy and Secure AI for Networks | Trustworthiness, robustness, calibration, governance, human oversight, secure deployment | Engineering trust in AI-enabled networking |
| 14 | Integration, Case Studies, and Student Presentations | End-to-end system view, case studies, project presentations, future directions | Course synthesis and final integration |

---

## Weekly Structure

A typical week may include:

- one main lecture
- one discussion session or paper discussion
- one lab, exercise, or project-related activity

Depending on the course format, some weeks may place more emphasis on:
- conceptual foundations
- research paper reading
- hands-on implementation
- project development

---

## Suggested Milestones

| Week | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Course orientation and technical setup |
| 3–5 | Early labs and ML/networking foundations |
| 6–8 | Topic exploration for final project |
| 9 | Final project proposal submission |
| 10–11 | Project progress checkpoint |
| 12–13 | Final implementation, evaluation, and report preparation |
| 14 | Final presentation and project submission |

---

## Reading and Preparation

Students are expected to:

- review lecture materials before class when applicable
- complete assigned readings or paper discussions
- participate in labs and technical exercises
- prepare progressively for the final project

---

## Notes

- The schedule may be refined during the semester depending on course pace and class progress.
- Some weeks may include updated readings from recent papers in AI for networking, AI for cybersecurity, and LLM-based operations.
- Project-related guidance will become more detailed in the second half of the semester.
