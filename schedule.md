# Course Schedule

## AI-Augmented and AI-Attacked Computer Networks

This page provides the weekly schedule for the course.

## Weekly Structure

A typical week may include:

- one main lecture
- one discussion session or paper discussion
- one lab, exercise, or project-related activity

Depending on the course format, some weeks may place more emphasis on:
- conceptual foundations
- research paper reading
- hands-on implementation
- project development

---

## Suggested Milestones

| Week | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Course orientation and technical setup |
| 3–5 | Early labs and ML/networking foundations |
| 6–8 | Topic exploration for final project |
| 9 | Final project proposal submission |
| 10–11 | Project progress checkpoint |
| 12–13 | Final implementation, evaluation, and report preparation |
| 14 | Final presentation and project submission |

---

## Reading and Preparation

Students are expected to:

- review lecture materials before class when applicable
- complete assigned readings or paper discussions
- participate in labs and technical exercises
- prepare progressively for the final project

## Notes

- The schedule may be refined during the semester depending on course pace and class progress.
- Some weeks may include updated readings from recent papers in AI for networking, AI for cybersecurity, and LLM-based operations.
- Project-related guidance will become more detailed in the second half of the semester.
