## 10. Recommended Reading Strategy for Students

A good reading strategy for this course is:

### First layer: foundations
Start with textbooks and broad surveys to build core understanding.

### Second layer: focused surveys
Read one or two survey papers for each weekly topic.

### Third layer: recent papers
Read selected recent papers to understand current methods, limitations, and open problems.

### Fourth layer: critical comparison
Compare:
- problem setting
- dataset or testbed
- model choice
- baseline strength
- evaluation design
- limitations and realism

---

## 11. How to Use These References

Students should use the references in three ways:

1. **To understand the fundamentals**  
   Textbooks and broad surveys should be used for conceptual understanding.

2. **To support assignments and labs**  
   Weekly readings will help connect theory to implementation.

3. **To support the final project**  
   Students should use recent papers and surveys to position their project and justify their technical choices.
